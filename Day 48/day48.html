What the scores mean

Each criterion score (0–100) is a Compare & Decide composite — not a third-party index — built directly from the sourced figures shown in each laptop's detail panel and the sources list below. Composites were used only where a single directly-comparable published number didn't exist across all four laptops (this applies to Display, Ports, and part of Build Quality). Where one clean sourced number existed for all four machines — Geekbench 6 Multi-Core for performance — that number was used with no adjustment.

Battery life

Vendor-claimed hours (Apple, Framework, Microsoft, HP) are shown for reference, but the ranking uses independent/real-world test results where available, since vendor claims are measured under best-case conditions (e.g. dimmed screen, video looping, Wi-Fi off). MacBook Pro 14 uses TurboVS's rated 20-hour figure; HP Spectre x360 uses PCWorld's measured 11-hour video-loop result. Framework Laptop 13 Pro and Surface Laptop 6 real-world figures come from ranges reported by reviewers (Vibetric: 12–16h; iFeeltech: 6–8h) rather than a single lab number, so the midpoint of each range was used and flagged EST.

Build quality & repairability

Framework Laptop 13 Pro (10/10) and the MacBook Pro 14 (4/10) scores trace to iFixit teardown data as reported by PC Gamer and a 2026 repairability retrospective. No iFixit numeric teardown score for the specific Surface Laptop 6 (2024, Intel) or the current HP Spectre x360 14 (2024, Core Ultra) configuration turned up in this research — iFixit's most recent Surface teardown covers the different Surface Laptop 7 (Snapdragon, 8/10), and coverage of the Spectre x360 only described its repairability qualitatively (SSD swappable, RAM soldered). Both of those two scores are therefore editorial estimates EST, positioned using the qualitative descriptions in their respective sources rather than a hard number.

Conflict resolved: HP's marketed "up to 18 hours" of Spectre x360 battery life sits well above PCWorld's measured 11 hours and Techozea's real-world 10–11 hours. The two independent tests agree closely with each other, so the app favors that figure over the vendor claim, which is shown alongside it for transparency.
Conflict resolved: Surface Laptop 6's Microsoft-rated "up to 18.5 hours" is far above iFeeltech's 2026 real-world estimate of 6–8 hours for typical business workflows (attributed to the Meteor Lake platform's average efficiency). The independent figure was used for ranking; the vendor figure is shown for reference.

Performance

Geekbench 6 Multi-Core scores for each laptop's mid-tier configuration were pulled from NanoReview's aggregated Geekbench Browser samples, which was the only source with directly comparable figures for all four machines' actual processors (Apple M5 Pro, Intel Core Ultra X7 358H, Intel Core Ultra 7 165H, Intel Core Ultra 7 155H).

Display

No single third-party index scores all four displays on the same scale, so this composite combines resolution (pixel count), confirmed panel technology (OLED, mini-LED XDR, or LCD), and confirmed refresh rate, each drawn from the cited manufacturer or review source. HP's Spectre x360 OLED panel and Apple's mini-LED XDR panel score highest on color/contrast capability; Surface Laptop 6's 3:2 PixelSense panel scores lowest mainly on pixel density and the absence of a published high-refresh or HDR spec in the sources found.

Ports & connectivity

Scored by port count, transfer-standard speed (Thunderbolt 5 > Thunderbolt/USB4 > USB-C), and — for Framework specifically — its fully swappable Expansion Card system, which by design lets the user choose which ports are physically present. This flexibility was judged the strongest connectivity story of the four, per Framework's own product documentation and Notebookcheck's coverage.

Models used

Comparisons use each line's most current or most relevant mid-tier configuration as of July 2026: MacBook Pro 14" (M5 Pro, released March 2026); Framework Laptop 13 Pro (April 2026 redesign); Surface Laptop 6 for Business (2024, Intel Core Ultra H-series — the model the user specified, not the newer Snapdragon-based Surface Laptop 7/8); HP Spectre x360 14 (2024, Core Ultra 7 155H — HP has begun rebranding this line to "OmniBook Ultra" but the Spectre x360 14 remains sold and supported as of this research).
