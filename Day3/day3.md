**Objective**
Learn how assigning different roles (personas) to Claude changes the quality, focus, and style of responses.
**Question Used**
"How can I launch a successful AI startup in 2026?"
**Prompt 1:** Without Any Role
How can I launch a successful AI startup in 2026?
**Output Summary**
General advice about identifying a problem.
Building an MVP.
Finding customers.
Marketing and scaling.
Basic startup guidance.
**Observation**
The response was useful but generic and lacked deep strategic insights.
**Prompt 2:** Founder Persona
You are a successful startup founder who has built multiple AI companies.
How can I launch a successful AI startup in 2026?
**Output Summary**
Focused on market opportunities.
Validating customer pain points.
Revenue-first mindset.
Building lean teams.
Acquiring early customers.
Fundraising and scaling strategies.
**Observation**
The response was business-oriented and emphasized growth, customer validation, and profitability.
**Prompt 3:** Developer Persona
You are a senior AI software engineer with experience building scalable AI products.
How can I launch a successful AI startup in 2026?
**Output Summary**
Focused on technology stack.
AI model selection.
Infrastructure and deployment.
Security and scalability.
Product architecture.
Continuous improvement.
**Observation**
The response was technical and implementation-focused.
**Comparison**
Aspect	No Role	Founder Persona	Developer Persona
Focus	General	Business Strategy	Technical Execution
Audience	Everyone	Entrepreneurs	Developers
Depth	Moderate	High	High
Customer Focus	Medium	Very High	Low
Technical Details	Low	Medium	Very High
Revenue Strategy	Basic	Strong	Limited
**Key Learnings**
1. AI Adapts to Assigned Roles
Giving AI a role changes how it thinks and responds.
2. Better Context = Better Results
Role-based prompting provides context that improves response relevance.
3. Different Perspectives
The same question can generate completely different answers depending on the persona.
